Arnesh Banerjee

B.Tech CSE (Data Science) · Heritage Institute of Technology, Kolkata

I am an undergraduate researcher broadly interested in the safety, alignment, and reasoning capabilities of large language models. My current work spans understanding the limitations of LLMs in mathematical reasoning, analyzing historical revisionism in LLM outputs, curriculum-augmented reinforcement learning for improving reasoning in smaller models, and medical image segmentation using hybrid CNN-Transformer architectures. I also work on multi-agent reinforcement learning, including co-evolutionary simulations for autonomous systems with a focus on AI governance. Previously, I have worked with Prof. Debotosh Bhattacharjee (Jadavpur University) on breast thermography segmentation and with Dr. Arnob Ghosh (NJIT) on safe RLHF and LLM safety benchmarking. My work has been published at AGC 2026 and is available as pre-prints on arXiv.
